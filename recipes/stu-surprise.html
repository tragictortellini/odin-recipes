Stu Surprise

Description

What's the surprise? When the Stu Surprise was added to the Sims games, we were given no other info, so we may never know.

Nonetheless, it looks delicious! Fans have been trying to recreate it, and so here is a tentative recipe for it.

This recipe serves 4.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ pounds braising steak, cut into 2.5cm cubes
  • ½ cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 Tbsp. vegetable oil
  • 1 medium white onion, peeled and chopped
  • 1 medium celery stalk, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, peeled and minced
  • 2 Tbsp. tomato paste
  • ⅓ cup dry red wine
  • 2 medium carrots, peeled and cut into 1cm pieces
  • 2 large russet potatoes, peeled and cut into 1cm cubes
  • 1 can of french onion soup
  • 1 bay leaf
  • ½ tsp. ground black pepper

Steps

  1. In a larg zip-top bag, add beef and flour. Shake until beef cubes are coated in flour. Set aside.
  2. In a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottom pot with lid over medium-high heat, add oil.
  3. Once hot, remove coated beef cubes from bag and shake off excess flour, then add to pot, leaving about 1cm between each cube.
  4. Brown well on all sides, about 2 minutes per side. Transfer to a large plate and repeat browning with remaining beef, and transfer it to the same plate.
  5. Reduce heat to medium and add onion and celery. Cook until just tender, it should take about 2 minutes.
  6. Add garlic and tomato paste. Cook until garlic is very fragrant and tomato paste is darker in colour. It should again take around 2 minutes.
  7. Add wine and scrape bottom of the pan to loosen any brown bits, then let the wine reduce by half. It should take about 5 minutes.
  8. Return the beef to the pot along with any collected juices, then add remaining ingredients.
  9. Stir well, then bring to a boil over high heat and cover with lid, leaving one side slightly vented.
  10. Reduce the heat to low and let simmer for 2½ hours or until beef is fork-tener.
  11. Serve hot.
